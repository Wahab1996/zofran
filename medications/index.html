💊 Medications Reference

This section provides a comprehensive, evidence-based overview of commonly used medications in pediatrics and adults, according to the latest clinical guidelines including UpToDate, AAP, and ADA recommendations.

🧒 Pediatric Medications

🩺 Analgesics & Antipyretics

  • Paracetamol (Acetaminophen): 10–15 mg/kg/dose every 4–6 hours. Max: 75 mg/kg/day.
  • Ibuprofen: 5–10 mg/kg/dose every 6–8 hours. Max: 40 mg/kg/day. Use only if ≥6 months old.

🦠 Antibiotics

  • Amoxicillin: 45–90 mg/kg/day divided q12h. First-line for AOM, strep pharyngitis, pneumonia.
  • Amoxicillin/Clavulanate: Same dose. Used for resistant pathogens or recent antibiotic use.
  • Cefdinir: 14 mg/kg/day divided q12–24h. For penicillin allergy.
  • Azithromycin: 10 mg/kg (day 1), then 5 mg/kg qd ×4 days. Alternative in penicillin allergy.

💨 Respiratory (Asthma)

  • Albuterol: Nebulized 2.5 mg q4–6h. Inhaler: 2 puffs q4–6h prn.
  • ICS (Inhaled corticosteroids): Budesonide, Fluticasone — maintenance therapy.
  • Montelukast: 4 mg (1–5 yrs), 5 mg (6–14 yrs), 10 mg (≥15 yrs) once daily.

🏥 Emergencies

  • Epinephrine IM: 0.01 mg/kg (max 0.3 mg) q5–15 min prn — for anaphylaxis.
  • Ondansetron: 0.15 mg/kg/dose q8h prn — for nausea/vomiting.
  • Diphenhydramine: 1 mg/kg/dose q6h (max 50 mg/dose).

🧑 Adult Medications

🩺 Analgesics & Antipyretics

  • Paracetamol: 500–1000 mg q6h prn (max 4 g/day).
  • Ibuprofen: 200–400 mg q6–8h prn (max 3200 mg/day).

🦠 Antibiotics

  • Amoxicillin: 500–875 mg q8–12h — for pharyngitis, sinusitis, pneumonia.
  • Amoxicillin/Clavulanate: 875/125 mg q12h — resistant pathogens.
  • Doxycycline: 100 mg q12h — for atypical or respiratory infections.
  • Azithromycin: 500 mg day 1, then 250 mg daily ×4 days.

💨 Respiratory (Asthma & COPD)

  • Albuterol Inhaler: 2 puffs q4–6h prn.
  • ICS: Fluticasone, Budesonide — maintenance.
  • LABA: Salmeterol, Formoterol — add-on if poor control.

🏥 Emergencies

  • Epinephrine IM: 0.3–0.5 mg q5–15 min prn — for anaphylaxis.
  • Ondansetron: 4–8 mg q8h prn.
  • Diphenhydramine: 25–50 mg q6h prn.
⚠️ Note: All medication dosages must be tailored to patient age, weight, renal/hepatic function, and clinical context. Always cross-check with the latest clinical guidelines before prescribing.

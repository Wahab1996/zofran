📋 Diabetes Mellitus

Definition: Group of metabolic diseases characterized by chronic hyperglycemia due to insulin deficiency and/or resistance.

Main types: Type 1 (autoimmune, absolute insulin deficiency) & Type 2 (insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency).

Goal of management: Achieve glycemic targets, prevent complications, improve quality of life.

🔍 Epidemiology & Risk Factors
  • Type 1: ~5-10%, onset usually <20 years, HLA-DR3/4 associated.
  • Type 2: ~90-95%, onset usually >40 years, associated with obesity, sedentary lifestyle, family history.
🔍 Pathophysiology
  • Type 1: Autoimmune β-cell destruction → absolute insulin deficiency.
  • Type 2: Insulin resistance + progressive β-cell dysfunction → relative insulin deficiency.
🔍 Clinical Features
  • Classic: Polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia, weight loss.
  • Type 2 often asymptomatic or diagnosed on screening.
  • Acute presentations: DKA (T1DM), HHS (T2DM).
🔍 Diagnosis
  • Fasting glucose ≥126 mg/dL, or random ≥200 mg/dL with symptoms.
  • HbA1c ≥6.5% or 2-hour OGTT ≥200 mg/dL.
🔍 Management
  • Lifestyle: Diet, exercise, weight loss, smoking cessation.
  • Pharmacologic: Metformin first-line (T2DM), insulin required (T1DM).
  • Additional: GLP-1 RAs, SGLT2i, DPP-4 inhibitors, sulfonylureas.
  • Monitor for complications: retinal, renal, neuropathic, cardiovascular.
🔍 Complications
  • Acute: DKA, HHS, hypoglycemia.
  • Chronic: Microvascular (retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy) & macrovascular (CAD, stroke, PAD).

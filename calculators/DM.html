🩺 Diabetes Mellitus — Overview

📌 Definition

Diabetes Mellitus (DM) is a group of chronic metabolic diseases characterized by hyperglycemia.

  • Type 1 DM (T1DM): Autoimmune destruction of β-cells → absolute insulin deficiency.
  • Type 2 DM (T2DM): Insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency, associated with obesity & lifestyle.

📊 Diagnosis

  • Random glucose ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms
  • Fasting glucose ≥126 mg/dL
  • HbA1c ≥6.5%
  • OGTT 2h ≥200 mg/dL
  • Prediabetes: FPG 100-125, HbA1c 5.7–6.4%

🔷 Complications

  • Acute: DKA, HHS, severe hypoglycemia
  • Chronic Microvascular: nephropathy, retinopathy, neuropathy
  • Chronic Macrovascular: CAD, stroke, PAD

🩺 Management

Goals

  • HbA1c <7% (individualized)
  • FPG 80-130 mg/dL, postprandial <180 mg/dL

T1DM

  • Insulin therapy: 0.5 u/kg/day start
  • Consider insulin pump

T2DM

  • 1st line: Metformin
  • Add SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA, SU, TZD, or insulin if needed

Lifestyle

  • Smoking cessation
  • Weight loss ≥5%
  • Healthy diet, regular exercise

🩷 Follow-Up

  • Screen annually for microvascular & macrovascular complications
  • HbA1c every 3–6 months
  • Lipid profile, albuminuria, eye & foot exams annually

👶 Children

  • T1DM: Insulin pump or multiple injections
  • T2DM (≥10 years): Metformin ± insulin ± GLP-1 RA
